Recounting

A chicken quesadilla is a Mexican dish consisting of a tortilla filled primarily with cheese and sometimes meats, spices, and other fillings, and then cooked on a griddle or stove. Usually, a corn tortilla is used, but it can also be made with a flour tortilla.

There are many different variations of this dish around the world due to international experimentation, but this meal is traditionally served in Mexican restaurants.

Ingredients

  • 2 skinless, boneless half chicken breasts
  • 1/4 cup white wine
  • 2 tablespoons of chili powder
  • 1 red bell pepper
  • 8 corn tortillas
  • 10 kalamata olives, pitted and sliced
  • 5 mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 cups low-fat cheese
  • 1/2 cup fresh salsa or 1 tomato, chopped and seasoned with Tabasco sauce

Guacamole

  • 2 pinches salt
  • 2 small cloves garlic
  • 1 avocado
  • 1 spring cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon plain yogurt
  • 1 and 1/2 teaspoon freshly squeezed lime juice - (1/2 lime)
  • 2 tablespoon minced onion
  • 1/2 cup fresh salsa or 1 tomato, chopped and seasoned with Tabasco sauce

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 400 degrees Fahrenheit.
  2. Put the chicken breasts in a shallow glass baking dish.
  3. Pour the wine over them and sprinkle them with the remaining chili powder.
  4. Bake for 10 minutes, then turn the chicken over and sprinkle with the remaining chili powder.
  5. Bake for 10 more minutes.
  6. The chicken breasts should be tender but not pink.
  7. Let them cool and then slice into strips.
  8. Pour the leftover baking juices into a covered plastic dish and refrigerate.
  9. Broil the red bell pepper on a shallow baking pan, turning it so that all sides become brown and blistered.
  10. Remove from the oven and drop it into a small brown bag and twist closed.
  11. When completely cooled, peel the skin off, scrape out the seeds, and slice the pepper into strips.
  12. Arrange the tortillas on the baking pan and evenly distribute the chicken, pepper strips, olives, and mushrooms on.
  13. Sprinkle 4 tablespoons of cheese on top of each tortilla and bake until the chese melts, usualy 5-10 minutes.
  14. Bake longer for crispy tortillas.

Guacamole

  1. Meanwhile, make the guacamole: sprinkle some salt into a bowl and mash the garlic against it with a fork.
  2. The grains of the salt will help to mash it up.
  3. Using the same fork, mash the avocado in the same bowl.
  4. Add the cilantro, yogurt, lime, onions, and pepper.
  5. Serve immediately.
  6. Serve the questions hot with the guacamole and the salsa alongside.
